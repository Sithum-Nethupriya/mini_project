Weather in Los Angeles

Los Angeles enjoys a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. The city experiences plenty of sunshine throughout the year, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and tourists alike.

Annual Weather Overview

On average, Los Angeles experiences 284 sunny days each year. Rainfall typically occurs during the winter months, while summers remain mostly dry.

Temperature Range

The average high temperature in the summer months (June to September) is around 29°C, while winter months (December to February) see average lows of 9°C.

Rainfall

Los Angeles receives an average of 15 inches (38 cm) of rain per year, with most precipitation occurring from November through March.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring (March - May): Pleasant temperatures and blooming flora make it an ideal time to explore the city's outdoor attractions.

Summer (June - August): Warm and dry conditions are perfect for beach visits, though inland areas get quite hot.

Fall (September - November): Temperatures remain warm, and the weather is often clear and calm.

Winter (December - February): Cooler temperatures and occasional rainfall create a mild winter experience.

Special Weather Conditions

Los Angeles occasionally experiences weather phenomena such as:

Santa Ana Winds: Hot, dry winds that can increase wildfire risks during fall.

Marine Layer: A coastal fog that often occurs in the morning but burns off by midday.
