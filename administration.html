Main Administrative buildings in LA

Los Angeles' administrative buildings are central hubs for the city's governance, housing key local, state, and federal agencies that manage public services, law enforcement, and urban development.

Prominent Administrative Buildings

Los Angeles City Hall

The central hub of LA's government, housing the Mayor's office and City Council chambers.

Kenneth Hahn Hall of Administration

Oversees Los Angeles County's governance and public services.

Edward R. Roybal Federal Building and United States Courthouse

Handles federal court cases and administrative functions.

Caltrans District 7 Headquarters

Manages transportation planning and infrastructure development.

Clara Shortridge Foltz Criminal Justice Center

A key venue for legal proceedings and criminal justice.

Wilshire Federal Building

A federal office building housing various agencies.

San Pedro Municipal Ferry Building

A historic building supporting ferry operations and municipal services.


Law Enforcement and Emergency Coordination

Los Angeles Fire Department Headquarters

Coordinates fire safety, emergency response, and disaster preparedness across the city.

Los Angeles Police Department Headquarters

Coordinates law enforcement efforts and public safety initiatives.

Emergency Operations Center (EOC)

The central hub for disaster management and emergency response coordination during crises.


Medical Facilities

Los Angeles County+USC Medical Center

One of the largest public hospitals, providing trauma and emergency care.

Cedars-Sinai Medical Center

A renowned private hospital known for specialized care and medical research.

Harbor-UCLA Medical Center

Offers emergency services and serves as a teaching hospital.
