Geographical Location

This map shows the position of Los Angeles on the western coast of North America. LA’s location near the Pacific Ocean makes it a key hub for international trade and cultural exchange across the continent.

Significance in the US



Positioned in the southwestern part of the United States, Los Angeles is one of the largest cities in the country. It plays a significant role in national economics, entertainment, and transportation networks.

Surrounding Landscape



Pacific Ocean: To the west, offering beautiful beaches and coastal attractions.

San Gabriel Mountains: To the north, providing hiking trails and scenic views.

Santa Monica Mountains: Cutting across parts of the city and offering outdoor recreation.

Deserts: To the east, including the Mojave Desert, characterized by arid landscapes.

Largest city in California



Los Angeles is the largest city in California and serves as a critical cultural, economic, and transportation hub for the state. Situated in Southern California along the Pacific coast, LA connects the state to international trade routes, particularly through the Port of Los Angeles—the busiest port in the United States.

Natural Hazards



Due to its geography, Los Angeles is prone to certain natural hazards, including:

Earthquakes: The city is near the San Andreas Fault and experiences frequent seismic activity.

Wildfires: Particularly during dry, windy conditions in the surrounding hills and mountains.
